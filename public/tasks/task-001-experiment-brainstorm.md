# Experiment Design Challenge

For each prompt below, you are given a **scientific topic**, **background information**, and a **materials list** — just like a real Science Olympiad event. Your job is to design a testable experiment using only the provided materials.

For each prompt, write:
- A **statement of problem** (what you are investigating)
- A **hypothesis** (your prediction and reasoning)
- Your **independent variable** (what you change)
- Your **dependent variable** (what you measure)
- A brief **procedure** (3–5 sentences)

---

## Prompt 1 — Velocity

**Topic:** Velocity & Motion

**Background:** The velocity of an object is the rate of change of its position with respect to time — essentially, how fast the object gets from one point to the next. Velocity = Distance / Time. Velocity is what occurs when a force acts on an object to make it change position. Today, you are prospective automotive engineers. Your goal is to design an experiment that explores the concept of velocity.

**Materials:** 2 toy cars (different sizes), 3 rubber bands (varying sizes), cardboard, printer paper, paper towel, aluminum foil, 3 marbles, 3 wooden blocks, latex balloon, mass balance

*Design a testable experiment using these materials to explore velocity.*

## Prompt 2 — Reaction Time

**Topic:** Reaction Time & Human Senses

**Background:** Reaction time is the time it takes you to respond to a stimulus. When catching a dropped ruler, for example, your eye sees the ruler drop, sends a signal to the brain's visual cortex, which relays it to the motor cortex, then to the spinal cord, and finally to the hand muscles to catch it. Different senses — **visual** (seeing the ruler drop), **auditory** (hearing a cue when it drops), or **tactile** (feeling a touch when it drops) — may produce different reaction times.

**Materials:** Blindfold, stopwatch, reaction time ruler, lab stool, ear plugs

*Design a testable experiment using these materials. Which sense leads to the fastest reaction time?*
