# Qualitative Observations & Applications: Ink Separation

## Scenario

A team investigated how the **type of paper** affects the **separation of colors** in black marker ink. They tested three types: **coffee filters**, **paper towels**, and **copy paper**. For each type, they ran 5 strips using the same black washable marker.

---

## Experiment Description

The team cut 15 strips of paper (about 10 cm × 2 cm). They drew a pencil line **1.5 cm from the bottom** of each strip and placed a thick dot of black marker ink on the line. For each trial, they poured **tap water** into a plastic cup to about **1 cm deep**, taped the strip to a pencil, and rested it across the rim so the bottom touched the water but the **ink dot stayed above the water line**.

With the **coffee filter** strips, the water **climbed steadily**. After about 5 minutes, the ink separated into **bands of blue, purple, and a faint yellow-green** — bright and easy to see with clear space between them. The wet filter became **slightly see-through** and felt **soft and floppy** when removed.

With the **paper towel** strips, water moved **very quickly**, reaching the top in under 3 minutes. The color bands were **more spread out and faded**, with **fuzzy edges** that blurred together. The paper got **very soggy** and one strip **tore in half** when pulled from the cup during Trial 2, so they redid it.

With the **copy paper**, water barely climbed at all — only about **2 cm** after 10 minutes. The ink **spread sideways slightly** but stayed as a **dark smudge** without separating. The paper felt **stiff and damp** but did not get floppy.

The team measured from the pencil line to the water line on each strip using a ruler. On some strips the water line was **uneven**, so they estimated the middle. Cups near the window seemed to **dry out slightly faster** from the warm afternoon sun.
