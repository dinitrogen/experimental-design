# Qualitative Observations & Applications: Density

## Scenario

A team investigated how the **type of liquid** affects its **density**. They tested three household liquids: **corn syrup**, **water**, and **vegetable oil**. For each liquid, they measured the mass of exactly **50 mL** using a graduated cylinder and a digital kitchen scale. They ran 5 trials for each liquid.

---

## Experiment Description

The team set up with a **kitchen scale**, a **100 mL graduated cylinder**, three bottles of liquid, **paper towels**, and a **plastic tub** for spills. They placed the empty cylinder on the scale and pressed the **tare button** to zero it out. For each trial, they poured liquid to the **50 mL line**, placed it on the scale, and recorded the mass.

The **corn syrup** poured **very slowly** — thick and gooey, clinging to the **inside walls** of the cylinder. It was hard to tell if the level was exactly at 50 mL because a **thin coating stuck above the liquid line**. The syrup was **dark amber** with a **sweet smell**. Rinsing was slow — it left a **sticky residue**, and one team member got syrup on their fingers and had trouble gripping the cylinder.

The **water** poured **quickly and easily**. It was **perfectly clear** with a visible **curved meniscus**. They read at the **bottom of the curve**, but one student accidentally read the top during Trial 2. They had to **crouch down** each time to be eye-level with the mark.

The **vegetable oil** poured **smoothly but coated everything**. It was **pale yellow** with a **faint greasy smell**. The oil left a **slippery film** on the cylinder, and they had to wipe it between trials so it wouldn't slide on the scale. During Trial 4, a few drops **splashed onto the scale**. Even with soap, the cylinder still felt **slightly slick** after cleaning.

After finishing, one team member poured a little of each liquid into a **tall clear cup**. The corn syrup **sank to the bottom**, the water **sat in the middle**, and the oil **floated on top** — forming **three distinct layers** with sharp lines between them. Stirring made it cloudy, but the layers **separated back out** within a minute.
