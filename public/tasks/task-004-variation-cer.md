# Variation CER Practice

## Background

At the **State** and **Nationals** levels, your report must include a **Variation CER** — a Claim / Evidence / Reasoning paragraph that describes how the *spread* of your data changes across your independent variable levels.

The Variation CER focuses on **standard deviation** (and optionally IQR) to describe whether data is becoming more or less consistent as the IV changes.

---

## Scenario

A team investigated how **ramp angle** affects the **distance a marble rolls** after leaving the ramp. They collected 5 trials at each angle:

| Trial | 15° Ramp (cm) | 30° Ramp (cm) | 45° Ramp (cm) |
|:-----:|:--------------:|:--------------:|:--------------:|
| 1     | 38.2           | 74.5           | 101.8          |
| 2     | 40.1           | 81.3           | 115.2          |
| 3     | 39.4           | 77.8           | 107.4          |
| 4     | 41.0           | 83.6           | 112.9          |
| 5     | 38.8           | 79.2           | 110.1          |

### Pre-Calculated Statistics

| Statistic | 15° Ramp | 30° Ramp | 45° Ramp |
|:----------|:--------:|:--------:|:--------:|
| **Mean**  | 39.5 cm  | 79.3 cm  | 109.5 cm |
| **Variance (s²)** | 1.21 cm² | 11.56 cm² | 26.01 cm² |
| **Std Dev (s)** | 1.1 cm | 3.4 cm | 5.1 cm |
| **IQR**   | 1.9 cm   | 4.5 cm   | 8.1 cm   |

---

## Your Task

Using the data and statistics above, write a complete **Variation CER**. Refer to the [CER Quick Reference Guide](/study-guides/cer-guide) if you need a refresher on the format.
