## Statistics of Central Tendency

**Mean (Average)** вЂ” Sum of all values divided by the number of values. *Include units.*

$$\bar{x} = \frac{x_1 + x_2 + x_3}{3}$$

**Median (Middle Value)** вЂ” Sort values smallest в†’ largest; the middle value is the median. *Include units.*

**Mode (Most Frequent Value)** вЂ” Value that appears most often (often "no mode" with 3 trials). *Include units.*

---

## Statistics of Variation

**Minimum** вЂ” Smallest trial value. *Include units.*

**Maximum** вЂ” Largest trial value. *Include units.*

**Range** вЂ” Difference between the largest and smallest values. *Include units.*

$$\text{Range} = x_{\max} - x_{\min}$$

## Experimental Error Types

| Error Type | Specific Error (Example) | Effect on Results (Example) |
|:----------:|:------------------------:|:---------------------------:|
| **Random Error** | A student hand-timing a ball rolling down a ramp gets 2.3 s, 2.5 s, and 2.1 s for the same distance | The three time values are scattered around the true time, making the mean slightly inaccurate and increasing the range |
| **Procedural Error** | A student forgets to release the ball from the marked start line and instead releases it 5 cm lower each trial | All distance measurements are shorter than they should be; the data appears consistent but the mean is shifted below the true value |
| **Systematic Error** | A ramp has an unnoticed curve that slows the ball at the bottom during every trial | Every recorded time is slightly too long; all three trials are shifted higher than the true value by roughly the same amount |
