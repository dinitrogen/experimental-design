# Teacher Guide — Sugar Solubility Practice Event

---

## Materials Prep Checklist

- [ ] ~200g granulated sugar (a standard bag is 907g — plenty)
- [ ] ~2L room-temperature water (fill ahead of time so it's truly room temp, not cold from tap)
- [ ] 12 plastic cups (9 oz / 266 mL) — enough for 9 trials + extras
- [ ] 3 plastic spoons
- [ ] Kitchen scale (gram precision)
- [ ] Graduated cylinder or liquid measuring cup (mL markings)
- [ ] Stopwatch or phone timer
- [ ] Calculator
- [ ] Paper towels for cleanup
- [ ] Blank report forms (or lined paper)
- [ ] Pens/pencils

**Setup time:** ~10 min. Pre-measure nothing — students should measure as part of their procedure.

---

## Likely Experiment Approaches

| Approach (IV) | DV | Feasibility | Notes |
|:--------------|:---|:-----------:|:------|
| **Amount of sugar** (vary grams, constant water volume) | Time to dissolve | ✅ Best option | Most intuitive; easy to control; clear results |
| **Amount of water** (vary mL, constant sugar amount) | Time to dissolve | ✅ Good | Works well — more water = faster dissolution |
| **Stirring vs. no stirring** | Time to dissolve | ⚠️ Tricky | Hard to quantify "stirring speed" as an IV with 3+ numeric levels |
| **Water temperature** | Time to dissolve | ❌ Not possible | Materials only include room-temp water |

## Dissolution Time Estimates

### Approach A: Vary sugar amount (200 mL water, steady stirring)

| Sugar (g) | Approx. Time to Dissolve |
|:---------:|:------------------------:|
| 5 (~1 tsp) | 15–30 sec |
| 10 (~2 tsp) | 30–60 sec |
| 15 (~1 tbsp) | 45–90 sec |
| 20 (~1.5 tbsp) | 60–120 sec |
| 25 (~2 tbsp) | 90–180 sec |

**Recommended IV levels:** 5g, 15g, 25g (clear spread, all under 3 min)

### Approach B: Vary water volume (10g sugar, steady stirring)

| Water (mL) | Approx. Time to Dissolve |
|:----------:|:------------------------:|
| 50 | 90–150 sec |
| 100 | 45–90 sec |
| 150 | 30–60 sec |
| 200 | 20–45 sec |

**Recommended IV levels:** 50 mL, 100 mL, 200 mL (all under 3 min)

## Timing Budget (50 min total)

| Phase | Time | What Students Should Be Doing |
|:-----:|:----:|:------------------------------|
| Planning | 0–5 min | Read prompt, discuss, agree on hypothesis & procedure |
| Experimentation | 5–25 min | Run 9+ trials (~2 min each avg + setup/cleanup between) |
| Write-up | 25–45 min | Split work per team strategy roles |
| Review | 45–50 min | Check all sections, units, labels |

---

## Potential Pitfalls to Watch For

| Problem | What to Do |
|:--------|:-----------|
| Students use too little water (< 50 mL) with lots of sugar | Nudge them: "Will your sugar fully dissolve in that amount?" |
| Students can't agree on what "fully dissolved" means | Suggest: "No visible crystals when you stop stirring for 5 seconds" |
| Stirring inconsistency between trials | Remind them to control stirring (same person, same speed, same spoon) |
| Students try to heat the water | Redirect — only room-temp water is available |
| Running out of time on trials | After 20 min, suggest they move to write-up with whatever data they have |

---

## Expected Results Summary (for grading reference)

**If IV = sugar amount (5g, 15g, 25g in 200 mL water):**

| Sugar (g) | Expected Mean Time (sec) | Expected Trend |
|:---------:|:------------------------:|:--------------:|
| 5 | ~20 | fastest |
| 15 | ~65 | middle |
| 25 | ~135 | slowest |

- **Data Trend:** Positive correlation — more sugar → longer dissolution time
- **Variation:** Standard deviation likely increases with more sugar (more variability at higher amounts)
- **Outliers:** Unlikely with careful procedure, but possible if stirring is inconsistent
- **Conclusion:** Hypothesis supported — recommend smaller sugar quantities in flavor packets for faster dissolving, or instruct consumers to stir
