## 1. Data Trend

**Claim:** There is a [positive / negative] correlation between [DV] and [IV].

**Evidence:** The line of best fit when plotting the mean for each IV level against the value of each IV level is: $y = mx + b$ → $y =$ [m]$x +$ [b].

**Reasoning:** Since the slope of the line of best fit is [m], which is [positive / negative], the [DV] [increases / decreases] as the [IV] increases. Therefore, there is a [positive / negative] correlation between [DV] and [IV].

> **Finding m and b from your graph:** Pick two points on your line of best fit (not data points — points *on the line*). Calculate slope: $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$. Then find b by reading where the line crosses the y-axis, or solve $b = y_1 - m \cdot x_1$.

## 2. Variation

**Claim:** As the [IV] increased, the variation of the dataset for each IV level [increased / decreased].

**Evidence:** The standard deviation for [IV level 1] is [σ₁]. The standard deviation for [IV level 2] is [σ₂]. The standard deviation for [IV level 3] is [σ₃].

**Reasoning:** The standard deviation of each dataset [increases / decreases] as the [IV] increases. Since standard deviation indicates how far data points are typically from the mean, this [increase / decrease] indicates that the data points are becoming [more / less] spread out. Therefore, the increase in [IV] is [increasing / decreasing] the variability of each dataset.
