## Experimental Prompt

Design and conduct an experiment to investigate a factor that affects how sugar dissolves in water.

---

## Background information

You are drink designers working for a beverage company. Your product team wants to develop a sugar-based flavor packet that dissolves in water. Before you can design the packet, you need to understand the solubility properties of sugar in water. Your experiment's results and conclusions should be able to help your product team **design a flavor packet that dissolves in water in a reasonable amount of time.**

Your product team has told you that for good taste, the ideal sweetness is somewhere around **10–20 g** of sugar per cup of water — but they want data across a wider range so they can see the full trend.

**Tip:** 3 trials per IV level is sufficient — plan your time wisely!
**Tip:** Sugar's maximum solubility in room-temperature water is about **200 g per 100 mL** — well beyond what you should be testing.

---

## Materials List (use at least 3)

- Granulated sugar
- Room-temperature water
- Plastic cups
- Plastic spoons
- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Measuring cup
- Paper towels
- White cardstock
- Cupcake liners

#### You may also use your stopwatch, ruler and calculator.
